Kokayi Walker is a GRAMMY-nominated musician, Guggenheim Fellow, and Washington, DC-based multidisciplinary artist. An improvisational vocalist and producer appearing on more than 80 recordings, his work transforms Black diasporic narratives into participatory experiences across sound, film, and visual media.

As an author, creative strategist, and technologist, Kokayi bridges art, culture, and technology to explore new ways creators and communities can participate in digital economies.

## Ambassador Project

During his time with the Interledger community, Kokayi focused on connecting creatives, technologists, and thought leaders around the world. Helping to expand conversations around financial inclusion, culture, and emerging technology, Kokayi has supported and hosted the [FM Podcast](https://interledger.org/podcast), created by the Interledger Foundation.

## What Inspired His Work

_"I was inspired to work in digital financial inclusion after seeing how many brilliant creators and small entrepreneurs, especially in Black and diasporic communities, operate within systems never built for them."_

As both an artist and strategist, Kokayi has experienced firsthand how access can determine opportunity. He believes digital financial tools have the potential to increase transparency, expand participation, and support more equitable economic ecosystems.

## Vision for the Future

_"I imagine a world free of barriers to entry."_

For Kokayi, financial inclusion is ultimately about dignity, ownership, and ensuring that creators and communities can fully participate in building sustainable economic futures.
